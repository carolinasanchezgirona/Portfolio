Overview

This project serves as a personal archive of my development throughout the Biomedical Engineering degree. Rather than storing every assignment, I will curate the work that best represents my progress, the evolution of my technical skills and the connections I discover between engineering and clinical neuroscience.

Motivation

After several years working as a psychologist and neuropsychologist, I decided to begin a second degree in Biomedical Engineering. My goal is not simply to acquire technical knowledge but to integrate engineering principles into the study of cognition, medical technology and neurological disorders. I believe the future of brain health will increasingly depend on interdisciplinary collaboration between clinicians, engineers and data scientists.

Objectives

  • Document the evolution of my engineering knowledge.
  • Create a public archive of selected academic work.
  • Connect engineering with clinical neuroscience.
  • Develop projects that combine psychology, technology and AI.
  • Share my learning process openly.
